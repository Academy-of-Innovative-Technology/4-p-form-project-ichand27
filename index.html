Risk Assessment:

A psychiatric risk assessment evaluates a patient’s risk to self (suicide, self-harm, neglect) and others (violence, exploitation) by analyzing history, current mental state, and intent. It uses interviews, standardized tools, and dynamic, time-sensitive review to identify protective factors and guide treatment planning. Key Components of Risk Assessment Risk to Self: Assessment of suicidal ideation, intent, planning, preparation, and history of self-harm. Risk to Others: Evaluation of violent history, threats, delusions, hallucinations, or impulsive behavior. Vulnerability & Self-Neglect: Assessing for exploitation by others, lack of basic needs, or severe neglect of health/safety. Contextual Factors: Reviewing substance abuse, medication compliance, social isolation, and living conditions. Protective Factors: Identifying coping skills, support systems, and reasons to live.
